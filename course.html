Computer Science

The Master's degree course in Computer Science dives deep into the theoretical modeling of real-world problems through algorithms and their practical applications. The foundation of this program lies in theoretical computer science, which explores how problems can be algorithmically represented and the complexity involved. Practical computer science then steps in to develop methods and concepts for solving these problems. Computer Engineering focuses on the hardware fundamentals, ensuring that the physical components can support the software solutions.

Economics

The English-language Master's degree program in Economics equips students with the analytical and methodological expertise needed to tackle key social challenges. This program is grounded in theory, research-focused, and spans all areas of economics. It emphasizes advanced mathematical, statistical, and econometric skills, enabling students to grasp complex economic models and evaluate the relevance and limitations of economic theories. Students delve into current topics and debates in economic research, gaining the opportunity to apply their knowledge to a broad spectrum of economic issues.

Mathematics

The Master's degree program in Mathematics equips students with in-depth scientific knowledge in key mathematical areas. Available specializations include "Algebra, Number Theory and Logic," "Analysis and Differential Equations," "Discrete Mathematics," "Geometry and Topology," "Numerics and Scientific Computing," and "Probability Theory and Stochastic Analysis." Students can start with introductory lecture modules and progress to advanced Master's lectures in various mathematical fields. Special graduate seminars offer a platform for presenting mathematical topics.

Geodetic Engineering

The Master's degree course in Geodetic Engineering is designed for students who have a first degree in geodesy and geoinformation or related fields such as computer science, mathematics, physics, geosciences, or electrical engineering. At the start, modules are provided to ensure all students have a common foundation of knowledge. The program then offers three specialized profiles: Mobile Sensor Technology and Robotics, Geodetic Earth System Sciences and Data Analysis, and Geoinformation and Spatial Development.

Ecumenical Studies

Ecumenical Studies is an international and interdisciplinary degree program taught in English, designed for students from diverse religious and cultural backgrounds. The standard program spans two semesters, but students can opt for the Extended Ecumenical Studies, which lasts four semesters for deeper exploration. The program assumes that active participation in the ecumenical movement requires specialized historical and theological knowledge. With its international and interdisciplinary focus, the goal is to contribute to the growing inter-religious dialogue and the relationship between church and society.

Physics

The Master's degree course in Physics at Bonn offers specializations aligned with the university's research strengths in particle physics, photonics, and condensed matter. These areas of focus allow students to delve deeply into their chosen fields while also addressing interdisciplinary questions. This approach helps students identify and scientifically classify connections across different disciplines. Throughout the program, students learn to apply scientific methods and findings independently and responsibly. They develop the ability to critically assess their work and that of others.

Our Facilities

The University of Bonn provides modern and well-maintained facilities to enhance the student experience. Some of the key features include:

  • Libraries: Bonn has several well-stocked libraries, including the Main University Library, providing access to thousands of books, academic journals, and online resources.
  • Student lounges and study areas: There are several spots on campus where students can socialize, relax, or work collaboratively on projects.
  • Sports facilities: The university has state-of-the-art sports centers and offers programs in various sports, including football, tennis, swimming, and more. These facilities are available to all students at minimal costs.
  • Research centers: With its strong focus on research, Bonn has multiple labs and institutes for students pursuing advanced degrees, especially in the fields of natural sciences, engineering, and technology.
